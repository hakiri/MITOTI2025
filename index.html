The 1st International Workshop on Middleware for IT/OT Integration (MITOTI 2025)

IT/OT integration bridges the gap between Information Technology (IT) systems, which manage data and business processes, and Operational Technology (OT) systems, which control and monitor industrial operations. It is a foundational element to drive digital transformation across industries. This convergence enables real-time data sharing and collaboration between enterprise-level applications and physical machinery, leading to smarter decision-making, improved efficiency, enhanced productivity, greater operational transparency, and enhanced safety. Developing middleware for integrated solutions that bridge the digital and physical worlds unlocks the full potential of the Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) and paves the way for smarter, more resilient operations. Despite challenges like legacy systems and differing priorities, successful IT/OT integration is a cornerstone of modern, data-driven industrial environments.

The primary goal of this workshop is to address the challenges and present advanced and innovative algorithms, models, techniques, architectures, specifications, tools, and frameworks that bring diverse middleware technologies at the interaction of Information Technology (IT) and Operational Technology (OT). Contributions addressing both theoretical and practical applications, including but not limited to, the following topics are sought for submission:

  • - Recent developments and future perspective of middleware for IT/OT integration.
  • - Middleware for edge-cloud continuum.
  • - Software as a Service (SaaS) for OT.
  • - Serverless and Function-as-a-Service computing for OT.
  • - Middleware framework for robotics, ROS, and human-robot Interactions.
  • - Middleware for converged infrastructure and IoT environment.
  • - Platforms and frameworks for constraint devices.
  • - Orchestration of IoT devices.
  • - Monitoring and resource management of constraint devices.
  • - AI based techniques for IT/OT integration.
  • - IT-OT security issues.

Format (Half Day)
Since this is the first offering of such a workshop, we propose that the MITOTI'25 workshop be organized as a half day workshop. However, if we receive a higher number of submissions and expression of interest from the community, we will request an extension to a full day workshop. MITOTI'25 will comprise 1-2 keynote speeches followed by technical presentations. The workshop will end with a group discussion among the participants discussing future directions and topics of importance. Depending on the quality of the presentations and discussions, we may plan a special issue of a journal, such as at Elsevier Journal of Systems Architecture or IEEE Internet of Things.

Keynote Speakers:

  • - Martin Lehofer, Practice Manager - Industrial Data, Amazon. Role of SaaS in manufacturing operations. (Tentative)
  • - Chris Woods, Senior Key Expert, Siemens. Role of Web Assembly in IT/OT integration

Submission, Review, and Publication Process: MITOTI 2025 proceedings will be published in the ACM Digital Library. Authors are invited to submit original, unpublished research papers. Papers must be written in English and strictly following ACM SIGCONF style as prescribed on the main Middleware 2025 website. Papers are to be submitted through the HotCRP system.
Two types of submissions are accepted:

  • - Regular research papers: contributions should describe original work (up to 6 pages including all text, figures, references, and appendices).
  • - Short papers and position papers: Research in progress, tools presentations, and new ideas (up to 4 pages including all text, figures, references, and appendices).
Submitted papers will be evaluated according to their rigor, significance, originality, technical quality and exposition, by at least three distinct members of an international program committee. Organizers

Shashank Shekhar is a Senior Key Expert at Siemens Foundational Technologies, Princeton, New Jersey. He received his Ph.D. and M.S. in Computer Science from Vanderbilt University and has performed extensive research in the areas of platform, industrial IoT, computer networks, middleware and resource management of distributed systems. He has published papers in top journals and conferences and also has best paper award, book chapters and patents to his name. Prior to joining Siemens, he worked for Teradata, Hewlett Packard and Red Hat. His current research focuses on resiliency, communication and performance aspects of AI/ ML platforms, industrial automation and control systems while driving the research for workload placement across edge and cloud resource spectrum for assuring performance of real-time and mission critical applications IT/OT systems.
Chris Woods is a Senior Key Expert at Siemens Foundational Technologies, Princeton, New Jersey. His research areas include runtimes and virtualization for distributed systems, focused on edge and cloud integration. Prior to joining Siemens Chris worked at Huawei research in Ireland, focusing on cloud and mobile 5G integration. He worked at Intel Labs Ireland conducting data center management research. Before entering industrial research, Chris worked at Microsoft Ireland where he led teams creating the initial infrastructure for the Azure cloud. Prior to this, Chris focused on embedded C++ and mobile phone operating systems. He has published papers on cloud infrastructure and has several patents in the space. He recently founded the Embedded Special Interest Group for WebAssembly and is supporting Siemens and other companies in its use in industrial settings.
Ajay Chhokra is a Research Scientist at the Institute for Software Integrated Systems affiliated with Vanderbilt University. His primary research interests are in the dynamic management of large-scale, highly distributed, safety-critical cyber-physical systems (CPS). He received his Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from Vanderbilt University, with a focus on model-based and data-driven techniques for diagnosing failures in cyber-physical energy systems. Before joining Vanderbilt, he worked as a senior research professional at Siemens, where he designed various testbeds to evaluate the resiliency of secondary and tertiary microgrid control algorithms, as well as explored the use of WebAssembly for real-time CPS use cases. His current research focuses on designing resilient and high-performance operational platforms for smart transit systems.
Aniruddha S Gokhale is a Full Professor of Computer Science and Engineering, with a primary appointment in the Dept of Computer Science, and a Senior Research Scientist at the Institute for Software Integrated Systems (ISIS) both at Vanderbilt University. His primary research interests are in blending software engineering principles (e.g., design patterns, modeling) with systems research to address challenging distributed systems problems. Currently his research focuses on addressing these challenges in the context of Cloud/Edge Computing, Internet of Things, 5G+ networking and Assuredly Autonomous Systems. He has been awarded the prestigious NSF CAREER award. He has also served as PI/Co-PI on a number of projects sponsored by NSF, DARPA, DoD and industry.
